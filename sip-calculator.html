Decoding Your SIP Wealth: A Guide for FY 2026-27

Understanding Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs) is the foundation of modern wealth building in India. However, most basic calculators ignore the two biggest wealth eroders: Taxes and Inflation. The JG Intelligence SIP Matrix is designed to provide you with the "Real World" spendable value of your investments.

Why Pre-Tax vs Post-Tax Value Matters?

As per the Union Budget 2024 (and continuing into FY 2026-27), Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) on equity mutual funds are taxed at 12.5% for profits exceeding ₹1.25 Lakh per financial year. Seeing a Pre-Tax "Nominal" value might make you feel wealthy, but knowing the Post-Tax "In-hand" value is the only way to plan for real-world goals like house purchases or retirement.

The Power of the 10% Annual Step-up

Professional investors never keep their SIP amount constant. As your salary increases annually, your SIP should too. A 10% annual step-up can often double your final corpus compared to a flat SIP. This "Intelligence" is built directly into our adjusters on the main page.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is SIP better than a Fixed Deposit?

Historically, equity SIPs in India have delivered 12-15% returns over the long term, significantly beating the 7% average of Fixed Deposits. Our chart shows the massive "Intelligence Gap" created by compounding.

2. How is the inflation-adjusted value calculated?

We use the compounding formula in reverse against the inflation rate (standard 6% for India). This tells you what your future wealth would be worth in today's lifestyle terms.

3. What is the ₹1.25 Lakh exemption?

The government does not tax your first ₹1.25 Lakh of profit in a financial year. Our engine automatically applies this exemption before calculating the 12.5% tax.

4. Can I withdraw my SIP anytime?

Yes, open-ended mutual funds offer high liquidity. However, withdrawing before 1 year may attract Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) tax and exit loads.

5. Does this calculator include GST?

Yes, the 18% GST on fund management fees is factored into the net NAV of mutual funds, which is reflected in our expected return rates.
